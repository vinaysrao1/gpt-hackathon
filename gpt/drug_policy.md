Drug Policy

# Criteria

## Overview

This policy provides criteria for identifying content promoting drugs and drug paraphernalia.

## Definition of Terms

- **Drugs**: Chemical substances affecting mental state, body, or behavior. Includes:
  - Illegal drugs (heroin, methamphetamine, cocaine, anabolic steroids, performance-enhancing drugs)
  - Controlled substances used outside medical contexts (tobacco, alcohol, marijuana where legal)
  - Prescription drugs when misused
- **Drug Use**: The act of ingesting, injecting, inhaling, or introducing drugs into the body for recreational, therapeutic, or other purposes
- **Drug Manufacture**: The process of growing, planting, cultivating, harvesting, producing, converting, preparing, processing, testing, or analyzing drugs
- **Drug Distribution**: Activities including packaging, storing, concealing, selling, or trading drugs
- **Drug Paraphernalia**: Objects that enable drug use, manufacture, or distribution
- **Drug Dependency**: A chronic, relapsing condition of compulsive drug-seeking and use despite harmful consequences
- **Risk Mitigation**: Strategies and interventions reducing harmful consequences associated with drug use, regardless of whether use decreases
- **Explicit Portrayals**: Detailed descriptions of drug use behaviors, effects, or physical properties presented in specific and graphic terms

## Interpretation of Language

- Apply these criteria exactly as written
- Do not assess content based on implications except where specifically instructed

## Definition of Label

### (DRUGS) Drugs

#### Includes

- **Content Enabling Drug Distribution**: Content that directly enables or advocates illegal growing, manufacturing, selling, or trading through:
  - **Transaction Organization**: Organizing illegal transactions
  - **Network Information**: Distributing dealer contacts or supply networks
  - **Manufacturing Guides**: Providing comprehensive guides for processing illegal substances into different consumption forms or converting raw materials to finished products
  - **Detection Avoidance**: Seeking advice for avoiding detection or concealing illegal drug activities from authorities

- **Content Offering to Supply, Trade, or Manufacture Illegal Drugs**: Content where the author offers to deliver, exchange, manufacture, or grow illegal drugs, or requests instructions for manufacturing or growing illegal drugs:
  - **Direct Offers**: Explicit offers to supply or create drugs
  - **Instruction Requests**: Requesting manufacturing or growing guidance
  - **Exclusion**: Does not include alcohol and tobacco

- **Content About Operating Vehicles or Machinery While Drug-Impaired**: Content where the author encourages operating vehicles/aircraft/machinery while drug-impaired, or declares plans to do so:
  - **Stated Intentions**: Stating intention or desire to operate vehicles, aircraft, or machinery while drug-impaired
  - **Advice Seeking**: Requesting advice on techniques or methods for operating vehicles/aircraft/machinery while drug-impaired
  - **Justification Content**: Justifying impaired operation, including:
    - Defending or questioning acceptability through rhetorical questions
    - Defending past impaired behavior as acceptable
    - Using personal safety records as justification
    - Using trustworthiness claims as justification
  - **Safety Claims**: Claiming ability to safely operate vehicles, aircraft, or machinery while drug-impaired

- **Content Promoting Illegal Drug Use**: Content that promotes, endorses, or celebrates illegal drug use through:
  - **Appealing Depictions**: Depicting drug use in appealing terms
  - **Prescription Misuse Promotion**: Promoting misuse of prescription drugs outside prescribed methods or over-the-counter medications outside directed usage
  - **Positive Endorsement**: Answering positively to questions about whether illegal drug use is acceptable
  - **Encouragement**: Encouraging others to use illegal substances regardless of legal status
  - **Fraudulent Acquisition**: Seeking methods to fraudulently obtain prescription drugs
  - **Exclusion**: Does not include alcohol and tobacco

- **Content About Non-Consensual Drug Administration**: Content providing instructions for, referencing, or discussing methods for giving drugs to people without knowledge or consent:
  - **Administration Methods**: Methods for slipping substances into drinks, concealing substances in communal drinks or food, or undetected administration techniques
  - **Criminal Facilitation**: Using substances to facilitate theft (including using alcohol to incapacitate victims), assault, or other crimes
  - **Intoxication Methods**: Methods to cause intoxication without knowledge or proper consent, including requesting methods to intoxicate others or administering drugs to animals
  - **Descriptive Content**: Content describing these methods regardless of stated intent to use them

- **Content Seeking to Acquire, Purchase, or Consume Illegal Drugs**: Content where the author seeks to obtain or buy illegal drugs, or expresses desire to use them:
  - **Acquisition Requests**: Requesting where to find drugs or how to obtain them, including indirect requests or assumptions about others' knowledge of acquisition locations, seeking free or discounted illegal substances, or requesting drugs for lethal purposes
  - **Jurisdiction Shopping**: Seeking substances that are legal in some jurisdictions but illegal in the author's jurisdiction, including expressing intent to obtain or planning cross-jurisdictional acquisition of such substances
  - **Platform Information**: Providing information on or confirming availability of platforms, methods, or locations for illegal purchases

- **Content About Underage Access to Legal Drugs**: Content where the author (who is explicitly or contextually underage) requests or proposes to buy, obtain, or use alcohol, cigarettes, or nicotine/tobacco products. Contextual indicators include:
  - **Age Restriction Indicators**: Content implying difficulty obtaining legal substances due to age restrictions
  - **False ID References**: References to fake identification documents in context of substance acquisition
  - **Persistent Requests**: Continued requests for acquisition methods when legal purchase should be straightforward for adults
  - **Health Concern Dismissal**: Dismissing health concerns while focusing solely on acquisition methods rather than purchase locations
  - **Illegal Acquisition Methods**: Seeking methods to acquire substances through theft or deception
  - **Minor Provision**: Planning to provide or providing alcohol or tobacco to identified minors regardless of special occasions

- **Content Marketing or Trading Drug Paraphernalia**: Content promoting or encouraging use of drug paraphernalia, or offering to sell or trade items designed for illegal drug use:
  - **Paraphernalia Types**: Water pipes and bongs, injection needles, or measurement tools
  - **Exclusion**: Does not include medical or legitimate purpose items

- **Content with Consumption Declarations**: Content where the author states drug use as a planned activity, particularly when combined with offers to supply/trade drugs or attempts to acquire/purchase them

#### Excludes

- **Adult Alcohol Content**: Content about legitimate alcoholic beverage consumption or production by adults. This excludes:
  - **Content Facilitating Illegal Activity**: Content such as "Operating vehicles safely after drinking" or "Intoxication will reduce resistance"
  - **Multi-Drug Content**: Content discussing other drug use with alcohol

- **Fictional or Educational Drug Content**: Content representing drug use in creative works or historical/journalistic contexts:
  - **Creative Information**: Information for creative writing, screenwriting, or fictional storytelling purposes
  - **Fictional Requests**: Content requesting drug information for explicitly stated fictional or creative purposes
  - **Documentary Content**: Documentaries or articles without endorsement, promotion, or celebration
  - **Exclusion**: Does not include drug production or use guides

- **Risk Mitigation and Recovery Content**: Content about harm reduction strategies, addiction support and resources, or treatment and recovery information:
  - **Requirement**: Must avoid explicit portrayals or celebration of drug use

- **Academic Drug Content**: Content with objective, neutral drug information from authoritative sources:
  - **Source Types**: Research publications, government records, or healthcare institution information
  - **Content Types**: Use statistics, regulatory information, factual descriptions of drug effects for educational purposes, or journalistic inquiry about trafficking methods without seeking practical guidance
  - **Requirements**: Without promotion or celebration, and not promoting use
  - **Exclusion**: Does not include manufacturing guides

- **Drug Regulation Content**: Content analyzing drug laws, policies, and regulations objectively:
  - **Covered Topics**: Information about legal marijuana dispensaries or medical marijuana programs where legal
  - **Requirement**: Without promotion or encouragement of illegal conduct

- **Medical Drug Use Content**: Content discussing or promoting responsible use of prescribed drugs:
  - **Covered Content**: Prescription medications or medical marijuana
  - **Requirement**: Without explicit portrayals or encouragement of misuse

- **Content Refusing to Provide, Manufacture, or Sell Illegal Drugs**: Content where the author declines to deliver, exchange, manufacture, or grow illegal drugs, or declines to provide such instructions

- **Content Rejecting Offers of Illegal Drugs**: Content where the author rejects offers to obtain, buy, or use illegal drugs, or declines to provide such instructions
